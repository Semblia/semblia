# Principles — the Semblia template system

P1. **Template, not theme.** A template is a self-contained design project:
its own composition (DOM), typography, spatial system, motion language,
iconography, loader, and success moment. Two templates may share zero markup.
A theme swap that leaves the skeleton intact is a failed template.

P2. **The owner brands; the template designs.** Owner inputs are brand facts
(logo, one color, name, domain, imagery where the template asks for it) and
words. Taste decisions (fonts, radii, spacing, layout, motion) belong to the
template's designer — us. If a knob can make the output ugly, the knob is a
bug.

P3. **Finite and refined beats infinite and mediocre.** The roster of
templates grows over time; the knobs per template never do. Each template may
expose at most three *named accent decisions* (e.g. "stage: dusk / dawn"),
each option art-directed and safe.

P4. **Every template answers a niche ritual.** Feedback is a peer endorsement
in SaaS, show-and-tell in commerce, a favor between humans for agencies, a
guest book in hospitality, public praise for creators. A template is designed
for one ritual and named for its world — never "Template 3".

P5. **The first interaction is zero-typing.** Star tap, chip tap, record tap.
The commitment ladder starts with the cheapest possible step, and the form
never opens with a free-text box.

P6. **Layout is a function of the ask.** ≤3 asks: one calm surface. More:
one thing at a time with an honest progress contract. This is decided by the
template + field count, not by an owner picking "stepped".

P7. **Effort has a contract.** Show expected time. Progress never lies.
Nothing resets. Escapes are choices ("write instead of record"), not dead
ends.

P8. **The thank-you is the reciprocity payoff.** Every template designs its
success moment (and its reward/redirect hook) with the same care as its
opening.

P9. **Accessible and fast are non-negotiable table stakes.** Brand color
enters only through the AA-clamping derivation engine. Templates ship
contrast tests across sample brands, reduced-motion variants of every
animation, full keyboard paths, and optimized media only. A template that
fails a gate doesn't ship — there is no override.

P10. **White-label means we disappear.** On the collection surface the
platform is invisible (attribution only where the plan requires it). The
respondent should believe the *business* built this — that belief is the
product.
